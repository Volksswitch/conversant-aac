The Response Palette — four structurally distinct responses
Stable left-to-right order  1 Preferred → 2 Dispreferred → 3 Initiative → 4 Repair
Preferred
The plain, affiliative second pair part — no hedging.
“Pretty good — mostly took it easy.”
Dispreferred
A decline/disagree in proper shape: softener + account.
“I’d love to, but I’m wiped out — this weekend?”
Initiative
Keeps the user from being purely responsive.
“Good — what did you get up to?”
Repair
A clarification on the partner’s turn.
“Sorry — dinner where?”
Position 1 is always the system’s best single guess; types stay in the same place every turn for motor familiarity.

Repair — who signals the trouble, and whose turn it was
Trouble is in the USER’s turn Trouble is in the PARTNER’s turn
Signaled by the USER
Self-initiated self-repair
The user decides their own last turn needs fixing.
→ Repair-of-self on the user’s own initiative: say it again, say it differently, or expand it.
Other-initiation, partner self-repairs
The user didn’t catch the partner.
→ “Pardon?” pushes a repair onto the stack and hands the turn back; the partner restates.
Signaled by the PARTNER
Other-initiated self-repair
The partner didn’t catch the user (“What?”).
→ Repair-of-self mode: the same say-again / rephrase / expand operations on the user’s prior turn. (The highest-leverage case.)
Self-repair by the partner
The partner corrects their own turn spontaneously.
→ No special mechanism: the restatement is simply re-captured and the options regenerate on the corrected utterance.
